Great instinct - you're right! Here's how to fix it:

Remove or make optional:

❌ Competitor websites - Most clients don't have this ready, and it's not essential
❌ Disliked elements - Asking what they DON'T want is harder than what they DO want
❌ Preferred fonts/style - Too technical for most clients
Keep these core questions (simplified):

✅ What does your business do?
✅ Who is your target audience?
✅ What's the main goal of your website? (leads, sales, info)
✅ Websites you like for inspiration (optional)
✅ Preferred colors (if any)
✅ Layout style (dropdown - easy to answer)
✅ How many pages do you need?
✅ Must-have features (contact form, gallery, etc.)
✅ Anything else we should know?
That's 9 questions, down from 12 - much less overwhelming!

Pro tips:

Mark "Websites you like" and "Preferred colors" as optional

Excellent point! Here's how to handle clients who don't know technical stuff:

Strategy: Guide them with examples instead of asking open questions

Instead of asking "How many pages?" Show them common options:

☐ Small site (Home, About, Services, Contact) - 4 pages
☐ Medium site (+ Portfolio, Testimonials, FAQ) - 7 pages
☐ Large site (+ Blog, Team, Multiple services) - 10+ pages
☐ Not sure - let's discuss!
Instead of "What style do you want?" Show visual examples:

☐ Clean & Professional (like Apple, Stripe)
☐ Bold & Colorful (like Nike, Coca-Cola)
☐ Elegant & Minimal (like luxury brands)
☐ Friendly & Approachable (like local businesses)
☐ Not sure - surprise me!
Instead of "What features do you need?" Give checkboxes:

☐ Contact form
☐ Photo gallery
☐ Online booking/scheduling
☐ Customer testimonials
☐ Blog
☐ Online store
☐ Social media links
☐ Not sure what I need
Make everything visual and example-based!

Even better for your test clients: Just ask 3 super simple questions:

What does your business do? (free text)
Show me 2-3 websites you like (any website, doesn't have to be competitors)
What do you want visitors to do on your site? (call you, buy something, learn about you, book an appointment)
Then YOU handle the rest:

You suggest the pages based on their business
You show them 2-3 design directions to choose from
You recommend features based on their goals
The questionnaire becomes a conversation starter, not a test they have to pass.
