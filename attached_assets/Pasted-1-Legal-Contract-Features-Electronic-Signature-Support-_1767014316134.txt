1. Legal & Contract Features
Electronic Signature Support

What it is: Allows clients to sign TOS, quotes, and agreements digitally.

How it works:

Signature fields embedded in PDFs or forms.

Each signature is timestamped to create an audit trail.

Optional PDF copies saved for client and company records.

Why it matters: Legally enforceable under U.S. law (E-SIGN Act + UETA) even if you’re an individual. Reduces paper handling and ensures acceptance is trackable.

Quote & Contract Linking

What it is: Approved quotes are stored and linked to the project.

How it works:

Approval in Duo automatically triggers the deposit/payment workflow.

Keeps contracts attached for easy reference.

Why it matters: Ensures all project documents are centrally stored and tied to the workflow, reducing disputes.

Deposit & Payment Tracking

What it is: Tracks deposits and final payments automatically.

How it works:

Payment confirmation required to move from Phase 3 → 4.

Phase 4 auto-locks until payment clears.

Why it matters: Protects you from delivering work without payment, and keeps the workflow consistent.

Cancellation & Refund Logging

What it is: Automatically logs cancellation requests.

How it works:

Captures timestamp of request.

Calculates fees automatically ($75 processing, $150 mid-project fee).

Why it matters: Provides a clear, auditable trail for refunds and fees, protecting both parties.

Warranty Period Tracker

What it is: Tracks the 25-day warranty for post-delivery support.

How it works:

Auto-starts countdown on final delivery.

Sends automated reminder emails when warranty begins and ends.

Why it matters: Keeps clients informed and ensures you honor your warranty without manual tracking.

2. Workflow & Project Management
Phase Milestone Timestamps

What it is: Automatic logging of each phase status change.

Why it matters: Generates accurate timelines for reporting and analytics, prevents disputes over delays.

Revision Requests

What it is: Captures client requests during Phase 7 (client review).

How it works:

Tag requests as “minor” (warranty-covered) or “major” (out-of-scope).

Why it matters: Helps manage scope creep and ensures warranty terms are followed.

On-Hold Notes

What it is: Tracks reasons for project pauses.

Why it matters: Keeps team and client aligned on resumption dates and expectations.

Automated Notifications

What it is: Sends emails when client action or internal action is required.

Why it matters: Ensures deadlines and approvals aren’t missed.

Progress Indicators

What it is: Shows % completion per project or phase.

Why it matters: Provides transparency for clients and internal tracking.

3. Client Communication & Support
Client Portal Messaging

What it is: Centralized comment system tied to phases.

Why it matters: All messages, files, and approvals are in one place, reducing email clutter.

Document/Resource Library

What it is: Central location for PDFs, guides, TOS, and instructions.

Why it matters: Clients have self-serve access, reducing repetitive questions.

Automated Monthly/Quarterly Check-ins

What it is: Automated emails regarding hosting, domain renewal, or support queries.

Why it matters: Keeps clients informed and prevents accidental service lapses.

4. Payment & Invoicing Features
Partial / Full Payment Options

What it is: Supports 50% deposit or full payment workflows.

Why it matters: Flexible payment processing while protecting your cash flow.

Payment Status Tracking

What it is: Shows “Pending,” “Cleared,” or “Failed” for each payment.

Why it matters: Allows your team and clients to see the payment state at a glance.

Automated Invoice Generation

What it is: Generates PDF invoices when quotes are approved or payments received.

Why it matters: Streamlines bookkeeping and provides official records for clients.

5. Reporting & Analytics
Phase Completion Reports

What it is: Exportable timelines showing approval, payment, and milestone dates.

Why it matters: Helps in client reporting and internal project audits.

Revenue Tracking

What it is: Compares deposits received vs. final payments.

Why it matters: Ensures financial visibility for your projects.

Warranty / Support Logs

What it is: Tracks which clients are under warranty and any minor changes made.

Why it matters: Maintains accountability and prevents disputes over warranty coverage.
